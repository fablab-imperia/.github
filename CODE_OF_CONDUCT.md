# Code of Conduct

Fablab Imperia is a community of makers, learners, and volunteers. Our projects live both online — in these repositories — and in the physical lab. This Code of Conduct applies to everyone who takes part, in either space, and its goal is simple: keep Fablab Imperia a place where people can learn, build, and share without being harmed or made unwelcome.

## Our commitment

We commit to making participation in our community a harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless of age, body size, visible or invisible disability, ethnicity, sex characteristics, gender identity and expression, level of experience, education, socioeconomic status, nationality, personal appearance, race, religion, or sexual identity and orientation.

We commit to acting and interacting in ways that contribute to an open, welcoming, diverse, inclusive, and healthy community.

## Our standards

Behavior that helps the community thrive:

- Being kind, patient, and welcoming — especially to newcomers and people learning
- Sharing knowledge freely and helping others learn (*peer learning* is how a fab lab works)
- Respecting differing opinions, viewpoints, and experiences
- Giving and gracefully accepting constructive feedback
- Taking responsibility for our mistakes, apologizing, and learning from them
- Focusing on what is best for the community and the projects, not just ourselves

Behavior that is not acceptable:

- Sexualized language or imagery, and sexual attention or advances of any kind
- Trolling, insulting or derogatory comments, and personal or political attacks
- Public or private harassment
- Publishing others' private information (such as a physical or email address) without their explicit permission
- Ignoring safety instructions in the lab, or acting in a way that endangers people or equipment
- Other conduct which could reasonably be considered inappropriate in a community setting

## Responsibilities in the lab and the projects

Fablab Imperia follows the spirit of the [Fab Charter](https://fabfoundation.org/global-community/#the-fab-charter). Taking part carries three shared responsibilities:

- **Safety** — not hurting people or machines. Follow safety instructions and ask when unsure.
- **Operations** — helping clean, maintain, and improve the lab and the shared work.
- **Knowledge** — contributing to documentation and instruction so others can learn from what you do.

Designs and processes developed here can be protected and used however their inventor chooses, but should remain available for others to learn from — see each repository's license.

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies within all Fablab Imperia project spaces — repositories, issues, pull requests, discussions, and any official communication channel — and in the physical lab and at events. It also applies when someone is officially representing the community in public spaces.

## Reporting

If you experience or witness unacceptable behavior, or have any other concern, please report it by contacting the association at **info@fablabimperia.org**. Reports will be handled with discretion and reviewed as promptly as our volunteer capacity allows. We respect the privacy and safety of anyone who reports an incident.

## Enforcement

Community leaders are responsible for clarifying and enforcing these standards and will take appropriate, fair corrective action in response to any behavior they deem inappropriate, threatening, offensive, or harmful. They may remove, edit, or reject contributions that are not aligned with this Code of Conduct, and will communicate reasons when appropriate.

Depending on the severity and pattern of a violation, responses may range from a private warning, to a temporary restriction from community spaces, to a permanent ban from the projects and the lab.
